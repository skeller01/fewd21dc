SANGEET

In Indian weddings, the sangeet is a party that takes place the evening before the ceremony and reception. Literally translated from Sanskrit, it means "sung together," as it is a lively celebration entailing song and dance. It is common for family members and friends to perform a group dance or to sing. Traditionally, women have mehndi (henna) applied to their hands during this event; it's often said that the darker the color of the henna, the stronger the husband-to-be's love is for his bride.

Dress Code: Cocktail Attire.

What we recommend: Come ready to dance, laugh, meet family and celebrate the beginning of our new life together.

CEREMONY AND RECEPTION

After a night of song and dance at the sangeet, the big day is here! Indian weddings start with the groom's procession, called the baraat. The groom, along with all his family and friends, will start in the lobby of the venue and make their way to the ceremony hall, where they will be greeted by the bride's family. The ceremony will commence and will be followed by a formal reception, dinner and dancing.

Dress Code: Black Tie Optional.

Transportation: Shuttle will be provided to guest hotels to the ceremony and reception venue between 3:15 PM and 3:45 PM. Return Transportation will be provided between 10 PM and 11 PM
